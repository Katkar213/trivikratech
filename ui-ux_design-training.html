FAQs — UI/UX Design Professional Training by Trivikra Tech

Q1: Do I need a design background to join?

No — this course starts from the fundamentals and builds up to intermediate industry skills. You’ll learn principles, tools, and workflows from scratch, with step-by-step mentor guidance and practical assignments.

Q2: What projects will I complete during training?

You’ll complete one mobile app capstone, one responsive web UI, and three full case studies that include research, wireframes, UI, prototypes, and usability test results — all portfolio-ready.

Q3: Are classes live? Will I get recordings?

Yes, all sessions are live and interactive. Recordings are provided for every class so you can revise or catch up at your own pace.

Q4: Which tools are covered in the course?

Core tools include Figma and FigJam. You’ll also get exposure to Miro/Whimsical, Protopie/Framer, Maze/Useberry, Smart Mockups, and developer-handoff tools like Figma Inspect or Zeplin. ChatGPT workflows for UX writing are included too.

Q5: Will I learn usability testing?

Yes — you’ll design tasks, run remote usability tests with real users (using Maze or similar), analyze results, and iterate prototypes based on findings.

Q6: Do you assist in job placements?

Yes — we provide portfolio reviews, mock interviews, resume/LinkedIn optimization, and guidance on reaching out to recruiters or freelancing clients. (Placement assistance varies by batch and region; please check current batch details.)

Q7: Can I join if I am studying or working?

Absolutely. We offer flexible schedules and recordings so you can learn part-time. Live session timings and batch options will be shared at counselling.

Q8: What system do I need to learn UI/UX?

A laptop with a stable internet connection is enough. Figma runs in the browser or desktop app; recommended: 8GB+ RAM and a modern browser for smooth performance.

Q9: Will I get a certificate?

Yes — students who complete the capstone and submit required deliverables receive a course completion certificate that outlines the skills and projects completed.

Q10: How do I enroll?

Click “Enroll Now” or “Get Free Counselling.” Our admissions team will schedule a quick call to assess your goals and suggest the best batch.

Q11: How long is the course duration?

The program runs for 3–4 months depending on project pace and chosen batch. Typical commitment: weekly live sessions + assignments and self-study.

Q12: Is this course beginner friendly?

Yes. The curriculum assumes no prior design tools experience and grows progressively from beginner to intermediate topics with hands-on projects.

Q13: What makes this course different from YouTube tutorials?

You get structured curriculum, live mentor feedback, peer reviews, graded projects, usability testing practice, and portfolio preparation — all focused on employability and real-world process, not just isolated tutorials.

Q14: In which language are the classes taught?

Classes are taught primarily in English with supportive explanations in Hindi where needed so learners understand concepts clearly.

Q15: Will I learn design systems?

Yes. You’ll create components, variants, tokens, and shared styles in Figma and document usage for scalable product design.

Q16: Will you help me create my Behance/Portfolio website?

Yes. We guide you through case study structure, visuals, asset preparation, and the actual publishing of portfolio pages on Behance or a simple portfolio site.

Q17: Can I switch to Product Design roles after this?

Yes — this course equips you with the core UX and UI skills needed to move toward product design. Additional product strategy or stakeholder experience will help for senior roles.

Q18: Is the course good for freelancers?

Definitely. We cover client discovery, proposals, pricing basics, delivery workflows, and how to package your case studies to win clients.

Q19: Will I work on team or solo projects?

Primarily individual portfolio projects. You’ll also have collaborative exercises during research and critique sessions to simulate team workflows.

Q20: Do you teach UX writing as well?

Yes. We cover microcopy, error messages, CTAs, empty states, and tone of voice, plus exercises to write concise, conversion-friendly UX text.
